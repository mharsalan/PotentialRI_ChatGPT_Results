
Based on the Inchcolm Project study and aligning it with various research impact dimensions, here are the potential research impacts, organized by type:

1. Academic Impact
Advancing Interdisciplinary Research: The project combines performance arts with video game design, creating new methodologies that can influence both fields. This interdisciplinary approach contributes to academic discourse in media studies, digital humanities, and performance art.
Innovative Methodologies in Creative Arts: The project showcases a novel way of blending game design mechanics with physical performance. This could lead to future academic explorations into how digital and physical worlds intersect, particularly in the realm of storytelling and cultural engagement.
2. Cultural and Social Impact
Cultural Preservation and Heritage: By using Inchcolm Island, a site with historical significance, as a live stage influenced by the video game "Dear Esther," the project revives and reinterprets cultural heritage, making it accessible through an innovative medium​(impact pathway)​(Common Research Impact …).
Public Engagement with Heritage Sites: The project fosters greater public awareness and appreciation of cultural heritage by reimagining historical spaces in ways that appeal to modern audiences, particularly gamers and performance enthusiasts​(Research Impact Knowled…).
Behavioral Change in Audience Experience: The interactive and immersive nature of the performance challenges traditional audience expectations and behaviors, encouraging new ways to engage with live art and digital media simultaneously​(Research Impact Knowled…).
3. Technological Impact
Cross-disciplinary Innovations: By integrating geo-tagged audio, live music, and installations with elements from video games, the project innovatively blends technology with live performance. This can inspire further technological advancements in gaming, virtual reality (VR), and augmented reality (AR) for performance art​(impact pathway)​(Research impact toolkit).
Inspiring Future Technological Research: The project sets a precedent for combining digital tools (e.g., game mechanics) with performance arts, possibly influencing technological development in interactive storytelling and digital art forms.
4. Environmental and Sustainability Impact
Sustainable Use of Natural and Cultural Resources: The performance’s integration with the natural landscape of Inchcolm Island promotes sustainable engagement with the environment. 5. Economic and Tourism Impact
Boosting Local Tourism and Cultural Economy: The performance has the potential to drive tourism to Inchcolm Island by creating a unique cultural event that draws visitors interested in both the island’s heritage and its innovative performance project. Creative Economy Growth: The blending of arts, culture, and digital media has implications for the creative economy. 6. Educational and Training Impact

Raising Public Awareness of Digital and Physical Intersections: The project bridges the gap between digital media (video games) and live performance, demonstrating to the public how digital and physical experiences can enhance one another, particularly in the arts and entertainment sectors​(impact pathway).
