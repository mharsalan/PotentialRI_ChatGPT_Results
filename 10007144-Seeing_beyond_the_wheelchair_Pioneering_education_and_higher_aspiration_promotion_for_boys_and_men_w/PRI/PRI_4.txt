After thoroughly reviewing the study "Troubling norms? Adults and teenagers with a life-limiting impairment in Denmark and England talk about their lives, support, and future plans" and aligning it with various Research Impact Dimensions, I have identified several potential impacts across academic, societal, and economic dimensions. Below is a detailed breakdown:

1. Academic Impact
The study contributes significantly to the understanding of disability, healthcare, and social policies, specifically regarding Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD). The findings:

Advance theoretical understanding: It offers new perspectives on how normative expectations about independence and life goals apply to people with life-limiting conditions.
Research methodology: The use of comparative focus groups from Denmark and England introduces a robust methodological approach that can influence future cross-cultural studies on healthcare and social support in life-limiting conditions​(4)​(Common Research Impact …)​(Research impact toolkit).
2. Social and Cultural Impact
Social Awareness and Advocacy: The study brings to light the differing levels of support and societal expectations in Denmark and England, highlighting gaps in care and the marginalization of people with DMD, particularly in England. This can shape advocacy efforts aimed at increasing support services​(Common Research Impact …)​(Research Impact Knowled…).
Cultural Norms and Social Change: By challenging normative notions of independence, the study could influence cultural perceptions about disability and foster more inclusive societal structures that recognize varying abilities and the right to support​(4)​(Research Impact Knowled…).
3. Health and Well-being Impact
Healthcare Improvement: The findings highlight the importance of healthcare models that integrate patient voices and lived experiences into care planning. Denmark’s healthcare model, which supports DMD patients with long-term care, may inspire improvements in the UK’s healthcare system​(impact pathway)​(Research Impact Knowled…).
Psychological and Social Well-being: The study addresses mental health and well-being concerns by advocating for more personalized, expectation-free approaches to supporting people with life-limiting conditions​(4)​(Common Research Impact …).
4. Policy Influence and Advocacy
Influence on Disability and Healthcare Policy: The study provides valuable insights that could inform both UK and Danish policymakers about the strengths and weaknesses of their support systems for individuals with life-limiting impairments. This may lead to reforms in social care policies and better transition services for young adults with DMD​(Common Research Impact …)​(Research impact toolkit).
International Policy Comparisons: The comparative nature of the research allows policymakers to adopt best practices from each country, potentially improving care and social support structures across Europe​(4)​(Research impact toolkit).
5. Economic Impact
Cost-efficiency in Healthcare: By highlighting the Danish system’s more comprehensive care for DMD patients, the study suggests that early and continuous support can prevent costly emergency interventions later. This can inform economic decisions regarding long-term investment in healthcare systems for chronic conditions​(impact pathway)​(Common Research Impact …).
6. Educational Impact
Impact on Curriculum Design: The study’s findings may influence the development of curricula in healthcare, social work, and disability studies, promoting a more nuanced understanding of life-limiting conditions and support systems​(Common Research Impact …)​(Research Impact Knowled…).
Professional Development: Healthcare professionals may benefit from insights into how expectations around independence affect their patients with DMD, encouraging more patient-centered approaches to treatment and care​(4)​(Research Impact Knowled…).
7. Technological Advancement
Assistive Technologies: Although not directly addressed, the study's emphasis on independence and lived experiences in Denmark highlights the role of assistive technologies and personal assistance services, which could drive further research into innovations that enhance the quality of life for individuals with complex impairments​(Research Impact Knowled…).
8. Equity and Inclusivity
Addressing Health Disparities: The study identifies disparities in the level of support between Denmark and England, particularly around socio-economic factors and access to care. 9. Resilience and Adaptability
Building Resilient Healthcare Systems: The Danish model of care discussed in the study demonstrates resilience by adapting to the long-term needs of DMD patients. The study encourages the UK to develop similar resilience in healthcare delivery systems for chronic and life-limiting conditions​(4)​(Common Research Impact …).
10. Sustainability and Ethical Considerations
Ethical Healthcare: The study advocates for ethical considerations in patient care, emphasizing the importance of incorporating patient autonomy and lived experiences in medical decision-making. 11. Community Engagement and Participation

12. Long-term Impact and Knowledge Dissemination
Knowledge Transfer: The findings could be disseminated internationally through academic publications, healthcare conferences, and policy workshops, influencing future research and healthcare practices worldwide​(impact pathway)​(Research impact toolkit).

The study holds broad potential for societal, academic, and policy-related impacts.
